Appendix A

The questions I know you're going to ask.

We know. Here are the answers.

They can add AI copilots to their workflow builders. That’s a faster human. AutoGrow eliminates the human. You can’t retrofit autonomy onto software designed around manual orchestration—same reason Blockbuster couldn’t become Netflix by adding a website.

The code is the distribution engine. The moat is the Cloud Intelligence layer—anonymized experiment outcomes across every customer. That corpus is proprietary, compounding, and impossible to replicate without the installed base. Open source gets us there faster.

The product debugs itself. It reads its own outcomes nightly and recompiles. At scale, a small CS team may make sense—but the ratio is 1:500, not 1:50.

It’s not projected. We ran AutoGrow against a live production database—66 tables, 77K users, $200K/month revenue. The 81.7% drop-off and $47K/mo leakage were diagnosed autonomously. Anyone can verify those funnel metrics.

Three layers. Confidence scoring holds low-conviction triggers for review. Nightly sweeps only promote variants that beat control on real outcomes. Send volume ramps gradually—10% → 25% → 50% → 100%. Aggressive about finding opportunities. Conservative about acting on them.

At one customer we’re a prototype. At 100 with Cloud Intelligence live, we’re a proprietary data asset that compounds. The acquisition math gets 10x better in 18 months.

Open source (devs find us), Jeevz case study (real numbers write their own content), and Product Hunt/HN launch. At $3K/mo managed, we need 30 customers for $1M ARR. Not 3,000.

That’s the first compilation pass. The LLM maps whatever it finds to business concepts—no predefined schema required. Tested on 66 tables. Messy data is the norm, and it’s exactly what makes this hard without the compiler approach.

18 months to 100 managed customers. $8M covers LLM compute, infrastructure, Cloud Intelligence buildout, and one hire if needed. At 100 customers: $3.6M ARR, 89% margins. Series A from data, not promises.

Trust. AutoGrow diagnoses beautifully. The risk is the last mile—getting customers to let it send autonomously. That’s why the Jeevz pilot with hard numbers matters. Once one case study exists, every customer after that is easier.
